Odumegwu Ojukwu C.

The man that saw tomorrow.

Below is how Odumegwu Ojukwu's life was spent.

  • 1933 was born in Zungeru, Northern part of Nigeria.
  • 1944 started his secondary education at CMS Grammar School, Lagos aged 10
  • 1946 He was sent to United KIngdom to continue his secondary eduction, first at Epsom College and later at Lincoln College, Oxford University, where he earned a master's degree in History.
  • 1956 He returned to Nigeria.
  • 1957 Ojukwu joined the civil service in Eastern Nigeria as an Administrative Officer at Udi, in present-day Enugu State. After two years of working with the colonial civil service and seeking to break away from his father's influence over his civil service career,[9] he left and joined the military initially enlisting as a non-commissioned officer (NCO) in Zaria.
  • 1958 From Zaria, he proceeded first, to the Royal West African Frontier Force Training School in Teshie, Ghana and next, to E aton Hall where he received his commission as a 2nd Lieutenant.
  • 1964 was promoted to Lieutenant-Colonel and posted to Kano, where he was in charge of the 5th Battalion of the Nigerian Army.
  • 1966 was appointed Military Governor of Eastern Region, by Major Gen. Aguiyi Ironsi.
  • 1967 He declared A soverign State of BIAFRA and became the Biafran president.
  • 1970 He fled to Ivory Coast for asylum as a result of Nigeria-Biafra civil war.
  • 1982 He was granted pardon to return to Nigeria by Nigerian president Shehu Aliyu Usman Shagari.
  • 1983 He was jailed by President BUhari, but was released same year.
  • 2003 He ran for Nigerian presidential election, but lost.
  • 2011 Dies in United kingdom after a brief illness.
